BMW, short for **Bayerische Motoren Werke AG** (Bavarian Motor Works), is one of the world’s most renowned automobile and motorcycle manufacturers. Founded in **1916** in Munich, Germany, BMW originally produced aircraft engines before transitioning to motorcycles and automobiles. Today, the company is recognized for combining **luxury, performance, and advanced engineering** in its vehicles. BMW’s brand identity is centered around **“The Ultimate Driving Machine,”** reflecting its commitment to dynamic driving experiences, precision engineering, and premium quality. The company’s iconic blue-and-white roundel logo symbolizes the Bavarian flag, representing its regional roots and aviation heritage. The BMW Group includes three major brands — **BMW, MINI, and Rolls-Royce** — each catering to different segments of the automotive market. BMW is known for sporty sedans and SUVs like the **3 Series, 5 Series, X5, and iX**, while MINI focuses on compact, stylish urban cars, and Rolls-Royce represents the pinnacle of luxury craftsmanship. In recent years, BMW has been a leader in **electrification and sustainable mobility** through its “**BMW i**” sub-brand, offering electric and hybrid models such as the **i4, i7, and iX3**. The company also invests heavily in **autonomous driving, digitalization, and eco-friendly manufacturing** to meet the future demands of mobility. BMW’s influence extends beyond vehicles — it’s a symbol of innovation, prestige, and driving pleasure worldwide. With factories and R&D centers across the globe, BMW continues to blend **German engineering excellence with modern technology** to shape the future of transportation.
